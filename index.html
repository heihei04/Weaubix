Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from our working holiday makers

Standard shifts are 8-10 hours per day, typically Monday to Friday with potential for overtime. We offer both day and afternoon shifts to accommodate different preferences.

All new employees receive comprehensive training including workplace safety, hygiene standards, and job-specific skills. Training is paid and typically lasts 1-2 weeks depending on the role.

No previous experience is required for entry-level positions. We provide all necessary training. However, any relevant experience may qualify you for higher positions or pay rates.

You'll need a valid passport, working holiday visa (subclass 417 or 462), Australian bank account, and Tax File Number (TFN). We can assist with some of these requirements after you're hired.

While we don't provide accommodation directly, we can recommend local housing options and connect you with other workers looking for housemates. Many of our employees share rental properties in the area.
